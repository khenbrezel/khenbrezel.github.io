About

Khen Brezel is a San Francisco-based visual artist whose practice investigates the tensions between the human figure and its interior life. Working primarily in acrylic, and occasionally in pencil and charcoal, Brezel constructs scenes that are at once theatrical and deeply personal, using layered brushwork and vibrant color to explore themes of vulnerability, identity, and queer embodiment. His male subjects appear not as archetypes but as emotional landscapes.

His recent work has been exhibited in institutions and galleries across California. His art resists simplification. Each piece, whether painting or drawing, is an invitation: to look longer, to read between gestures, to feel what is not said.

Working between acrylic, pencil, and charcoal, Brezel treats the male figure as a site of memory and projection. Surfaces hold fingerprints, erasures, and the soft afterimage of photographic light, so the work feels both immediate and constructed. The result is a body of work that asks how intimacy, identity, and belonging shift when the image returns to the hand.

Series Statement

Unarmored

Unarmored is a meditation on the masculine form, unclothed, yes, but more radically, unguarded. In this series, Khen Brezel presents a constellation of male figures: lounging, fragmented, interrupted by orchids, shadows, chairs, citrus. The gaze is quiet but charged, as if the paintings are less concerned with seduction than with the fragile architecture of presence. Every canvas is queer, sometimes overtly, sometimes through nuance, through coded gesture or color, but never didactic.

Rather than broadcasting identity, Brezel's figures inhabit it. There is ambiguity, vulnerability, beauty, and posture, but always a subtle defiance against how the male body has historically been posed and possessed. Bodies are not only seen; they seem to listen. The paintings unfold like rooms without doors: intimate, arresting, open to interpretation but never passive.

Current Exhibition

City Art Cooperative Gallery: February Group Exhibition

February 4 to 28, 2026. Opening reception Friday, February 6, 7 to 10pm. Curated by Colin Sjostedt.

Khen's presentation brings charcoal on canvas figures into dialogue with pencil studies of male nudes. The grouping, rendered in black, white, and subtle blue tones, reflects on queer art history, the male gaze, and the shifting ideals of beauty in contemporary culture.

The works also trace a conversation between media: photography's claim to realism and charcoal's tactile, imperfect surface. Erased traces, fingerprints, and the ghost of a camera flash remain visible, reminding us how perception changes when an image returns to the hand.
